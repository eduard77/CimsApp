# CLAIMS MANAGEMENT PLAN

**Document Reference:** [PRJ-CODE]-CLM-001
**Revision:** P01
**CDE Status:** Shared · Suitability: S3

> *Claims Management* is a distinct PMBOK Construction Extension knowledge area. It covers identification, quantification, prevention, and resolution of claims between contracting parties.

---

## 1. PRINCIPLES

- **Avoid where possible** — robust design, clear contracts, early communication
- **Notify promptly** — per contract time bars
- **Record contemporaneously** — daily records, photos, correspondence
- **Quantify transparently** — cost and programme evidence
- **Resolve at lowest level** — escalate only when necessary

---

## 2. CONTRACT FRAMEWORK

Refer to relevant contract clauses:
| Standard | Clause |
|---|---|
| NEC4 | Compensation Events (Cl. 60), Early Warning (Cl. 15) |
| JCT 2016 | Relevant Events (Cl. 2.29), Loss & Expense (Cl. 4.21) |
| FIDIC Red | Variations (Cl. 13), Claims (Cl. 20) |

---

## 3. CLAIM CATEGORIES

| Type | Description | Typical Cause |
|---|---|---|
| Extension of Time (EOT) | Programme impact | Adverse weather, client change, unforeseen conditions |
| Loss & Expense | Cost recovery | Prolongation, disruption, acceleration |
| Variation / Compensation Event | Scope or price change | Design change, changed site conditions |
| Defective works | Cost of remedy | Workmanship, materials |
| Counterclaim | Reverse direction | Liquidated damages, back-charges |

---

## 4. EARLY WARNING PROCESS

1. Party becomes aware of an event that could affect cost / time / quality
2. **Early Warning Notice** issued within contract period (usually 2 weeks)
3. Recorded in `/08_Risk_Management/06_Early_Warning_Notices/`
4. Risk Reduction Meeting convened
5. Actions agreed to mitigate or eliminate

---

## 5. CLAIM SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

Every formal claim must contain:
- Cause & effect narrative
- Contract clause relied upon
- Chronology of events
- Evidence (correspondence, RFIs, instructions, delay analysis)
- Quantum substantiation
- Compliance with time bars

## 7. DISPUTE RESOLUTION ESCALATION

1. **Negotiation** — Commercial team
2. **Senior-level negotiation** — PM → Directors
3. **Mediation** — Neutral third party
4. **Adjudication** (UK Construction Act) — 28-day process
5. **Arbitration / Litigation** — as last resort
