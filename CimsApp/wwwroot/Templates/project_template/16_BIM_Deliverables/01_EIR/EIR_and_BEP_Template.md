# EXCHANGE INFORMATION REQUIREMENTS (EIR)

**Document Reference:** [PRJ-CODE]-EIR-001
**Revision:** P01
**CDE Status:** Published · Suitability: S4
**Standard:** ISO 19650-2:2018 §5.2
**Issued by:** Appointing Party (Client)

---

## 1. PURPOSE

The EIR specifies the information the appointing party requires from appointed parties (consultants, contractors) in response to the Project Information Requirements (PIR) and Asset Information Requirements (AIR).

---

## 2. INFORMATION REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Information Purposes
*State why the information is needed — design decisions, permission applications, maintenance planning, etc.*

### 2.2 Information Containers
Expected information containers (models, drawings, schedules, reports) per stage:
| Stage | Deliverable | Format | LOD | LOI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| RIBA 2 | Concept architectural model | IFC, .rvt | 200 | Generic |
| RIBA 3 | Coordinated federated model | IFC | 300 | Type |
| RIBA 4 | Construction-ready models | IFC, native | 350 | Manufacturer |
| RIBA 5 | As-constructed models | IFC + COBie | 400 | Installed |
| RIBA 6 | AIM for handover | COBie + IFC | 500 | Operational |

### 2.3 Level of Information Need (ISO 19650-1)
Balance between:
- **Geometrical information** (Level of Detail — LOD)
- **Alphanumerical information** (Level of Information — LOI)
- **Documentation**

## 4. COMPETENCE REQUIREMENTS

Each appointed party must demonstrate competence in:
- BIM management (e.g. BSI PAS 1192 accredited)
- ISO 19650-2 workflows
- Software authoring tools relevant to the appointment
