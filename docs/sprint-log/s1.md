**Planned:** 2026-04-26 → 2026-05-17 (3 weeks)
**Roadmap reference:** PAFM-SD Ch 6.3, Phase 2
**Module DoD:** PAFM Appendix F.2

## Sprint goal

Deliver the Cost & Commercial module (PMBOK 8 Finance domain) on
the foundation laid by Sprint 0. Covers cost breakdown structure
(CBS), earned value management (EVM), variations / change orders,
payment certificates, and cashflow forecasting in the UK
construction context (NEC4 / JCT contractual conventions).

Inherits from S0:
- `ITenantContext` and the full query-filter scoping (every Cost
  entity is tenant-scoped via its `Project.AppointingPartyId`).
- Two-tier role model (ADR-0010): financial endpoints are
  candidates for `[Authorize(Roles=...)]` global gates plus
  per-project `HasMinimumRole` checks.
- Audit interceptor — every cost / variation / certificate write
  is automatically audited.
- Invitation-token registration so only authorised users land in
  a tenant.

## Scope decision required (resolve before T-S1-02)

The full F.2 spec does not fit one 3-week sprint at the formal
capacity calculation. Three honest paths:

**A. Cut scope to fit (recommended by PAFM-SD).** Defer the
following to fast-follow mini-sprints or v1.1 backlog, brought
back via formal change records:
- T-S1-10 Construction Act notices (6h) → fast-follow after
  T-S1-09 payment cert lands.
- T-S1-12 Final account schedule (4h) → S-late or v1.1.
- T-S1-14 MudBlazor 7 upgrade (4h) → defer to v1.1 (`B-013`
  candidate); the pre-existing warning is not blocking S1.
- Trim T-S1-08 Variations workflow from 6 states to 3
  (raise → approve → reject) for v1.0; defer assess / instruct /
  value / agree to v1.1. Saves ~6h.

This brings the estimate to 64h × 1.5 = 96h. Still over 72h but
within the S0-velocity-adjusted budget — see option C.

**B. Accept the overrun and extend the sprint.** PAFM-SD Ch 4 C-12
explicitly forbids this: *"I will time-box sprints per the
roadmap. Overrun forces a scope cut, not a sprint extension."*
This is a waiver against the discipline manual and would require
a written ADR per Ch 17.

**C. Re-base capacity against measured S0 velocity.** S0
retrospective recorded 49h of estimated work landed in 2 working
days under AI pairing. If 60-70% of that velocity persists, 126h
fits comfortably in 3 weeks. The retrospective explicitly warned
not to assume S0 velocity carries — but it could equally not be
half-discounted.

Recommended: **A**. Honest with the discipline. Defers genuinely
late-stage work (final account, statutory notices) and tech debt
(MudBlazor 7) without abandoning anything. Variations workflow
trim is the only F.2 cut and is justifiable because the four
deferred states (assess / instruct / value / agree) are mostly
process-tracking on top of the core raise→approve→reject
state-machine which the early cut already delivers.

Until this is resolved, T-S1-02 onwards are blocked.

## Top 3 risks (PAFM-SD Appendix E)

1. **NEC4 / JCT semantics are easy to get subtly wrong.** Payment
   certificate calculations differ between the two conventions
   (e.g. how retention is handled, when the activity-schedule
   resets). Mitigation: pick one convention as the v1.0 default
   (likely NEC4 given the construction sector), document the choice
   in an ADR, defer the alternative to v1.1.
2. **EVM rollup performance on large CBS trees.** A real construction
   project has 200-500 CBS items. Naïve N+1 queries on rollup will
   crawl. Mitigation: use EF Core's `Include` / projection from
   day 1, behavioural-test the rollup with a 500-item fixture
   before any UI work, profile.
3. **Variations workflow scope creep.** Real-world change-order
   processes have 5-10 stages including legal review, programme
   impact assessment, etc. Mitigation: deliver a 3-state machine
   (Raised → Approved/Rejected) for v1.0 and explicitly defer
   intermediate stages to v1.1 backlog with a change record if
   needed.
